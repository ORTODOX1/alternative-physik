The Killer Fact

Palladium. Atomic number 46. The Coulomb barrier for D-D fusion in Pd should be a single number, determined by nuclear charge. Standard Debye screening model predicts ~30 eV for any form of Pd.

Standard model prediction for ALL of these: ~30 eV.
Error: 900% to 60,000%. The model is broken.

The ONLY difference between these samples is how the metal was processed. Cold-rolling creates crystal defects — dislocations, vacancies, grain boundaries. The atomic number doesn't change. The electron configuration doesn't change. But the screening energy changes by 60×.

This is impossible if the Coulomb barrier is a fundamental nuclear constant.

What This Means

The Coulomb barrier is like electrical resistance.

Copper conducts electricity. Rubber doesn't. Not because of some "fundamental electron barrier" — but because of the material's crystal structure.

The same is true for nuclear fusion. Cold-rolled palladium lets deuterons approach 60× closer than annealed palladium. Not because the nuclear force changed — but because the crystal defects create channels that reduce the effective barrier.

Nuclear fusion is an engineering problem, not a fundamental physics barrier.

Implications for Energy

If the barrier is engineerable, fusion is engineerable.

Today we spend $30B+ on tokamaks trying to overcome a 400 keV "fundamental" barrier with brute force (100 million degrees). But if cold-rolling a piece of palladium reduces the effective barrier by 60×, then material science — not plasma physics — is the path to fusion.

The semiconductor revolution happened when we learned to engineer electron barriers in silicon. The energy revolution happens when we learn to engineer nuclear barriers in metals.
